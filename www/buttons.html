  • What is Adaptive IT?

    To succeed in an era when change is constant and the impact of technology ubiquitous, IT leaders must apply five principles that enable continuous change in the IT organization.
    These principles are easier to articulate than they are to live by day in and day out. To make the principles real, IT leaders must create an Adaptive IT organization that is capable of continuous change and can thrive in any environment, not just the environment for which it was designed. Adaptive IT organizations require fundamental changes in IT’s strategy, governance, delivery, and workforce.

  • Adaptive Strategy

    Adaptive strategy recognizes that IT’s role is context-based. IT needs to seamlessly shift between roles to help the enterprise extract full value from technology, regardless of where the ideas or money come from. This requires IT to gear up to play multiple roles, such as delivering, consulting, brokering, and coaching, often in parallel. IT can’t just operate at two-speeds; many different speeds and approaches are required to support the full range of circumstances IT may face.

    How We Can Help

    Shift Engagement Toward Coaching, Advising, and Brokering
    Spend Equal Time Engaging with Business Leaders and Employees
    Focus on IT’s Enduring Comparative Advantages
    Strike the Right Balance on When to Engage

  • Adaptive Governance and Delivery

    Adaptive governance and delivery is about optimizing for speed-to-market. Adaptive IT organizations increase speed-to-market through faster governance and the ability to dynamically reallocate investments to free up resources.

    Speed is not just about doing projects faster, but rather building in options and more quickly realizing benefits. Speed is enabled by test-and-learn, so that opportunities quickly get the right resources when value presents itself. Many progressive IT teams have found that the end-to-end-IT services model offers the best way to make governance and delivery adaptive.



    How We Can Help

    Accelerate Governance Clock Speed
    Dynamically Reallocate Resources
    Transition to an End-to-End IT Services Model

  • Adaptive Workforce

    An adaptive workforce continues to develop business-friendly competencies and behaviors, while simultaneously replenishing IT's technical edge. To build an adaptive workforce, IT staff must have:

    1. The desire to be adaptive and change as needed
    2. The skills to engage with business partners effectively
    3. The right functional expertise, including advanced knowledge of emerging technologies.

    To build an adaptive workforce, IT leaders first need to overcome roadblocks that arise from IT staff not having the right mindsets, competencies, and functional expertise. Unless IT employees can adjust, every role in IT can impede new technology projects.

    How We Can Help

    Expand IT’s Engagement Competencies
    Replenish Cutting Edge Technical Knowledge
    Develop a Climate of Openness in IT
